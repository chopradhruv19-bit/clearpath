Why we built ClearPath

Imagine receiving an official government letter about your home — and not understanding a single word of it. No one to call. No time to wait. A deadline you didn't even know existed.

This is the everyday reality for millions of immigrants and newcomers around the world. They are not lacking intelligence or willpower — they are lacking access. Access to clear information, in a language they understand, at the moment they need it most.

Government housing systems are built on paperwork. But that paperwork is written in complex legal English, filled with jargon, and assumes the reader already knows how the system works. For someone new to a country, it might as well be a foreign language — even if it's technically in English.

We built ClearPath because we believe that no family should lose their home simply because they couldn't understand a letter. AI should not just be a tool for the privileged — it should be a bridge for those who need it most.

Our goal

Turn confusion into clarity — so families can act, not panic.

Our belief

Access to clear information is a basic right, not a privilege.

Our promise

Plain language, real next steps — no legal jargon, ever.

    The problem we're solving

    Every year, thousands of immigrant families receive government housing letters they cannot fully understand. The language is legal, the deadlines are tight, and the consequences of missing them can be severe — including losing housing assistance entirely.

    These families are not struggling because they don't care. They're struggling because the system was never designed with them in mind.

    1 in 5

    U.S. residents speak a language other than English at home, yet most government letters arrive in English only.

    14 days

    The typical response window on a housing notice — barely enough time to find help, let alone act on it.

    Grade 16

    The average reading level of U.S. government documents — equivalent to a college senior. Most are written for lawyers, not families.
